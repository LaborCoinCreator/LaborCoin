## Purpose

The LaborCoin Treasury Module acts as the dedicated treasury distribution component of the LaborCoin ecosystem.

Its sole purpose is to receive treasury funds and execute distributions that have been approved through the LaborCoin governance process.

The Treasury Module functions as a constrained execution layer between governance decisions and treasury disbursements.

---

## Treasury Role

The Treasury Module receives funds from LaborCoin revenue sources, including protocol treasury allocations generated by the LaborCoin Exchange.

Funds held by the Treasury Module may only be distributed according to the rules defined by the governance system.

The Treasury Module does not create proposals, conduct voting, or determine treasury policy.

Its role is limited to executing approved treasury transfers.

## Distribution Process

Treasury distributions follow a structured governance process:

1. A proposal is submitted through LaborCoin Governance.
2. Members vote on the proposal.
3. The proposal satisfies all approval requirements.
4. The proposal enters the execution window.
5. Governance authorizes execution.
6. The Treasury Module transfers funds to the approved recipient.

This separation ensures that treasury execution remains subject to governance approval.

---

## Security Model

The Treasury Module is intentionally restricted in scope.

The contract cannot:

* Create proposals
* Conduct voting
* Modify governance rules
* Mint tokens
* Alter token supply
* Modify exchange behavior
* Independently distribute treasury funds

Only authorized governance actions may trigger treasury distributions.

This design minimizes the contract's attack surface and limits its authority to a narrowly defined function.

---

## Treasury Controls

Treasury transfers remain subject to the constraints defined by the governance system.

Examples may include:

* Proposal approval thresholds
* Participation requirements
* Execution windows
* Treasury distribution limits

The Treasury Module enforces execution while governance determines authorization.

---

## Transparency

All treasury distributions are permanently recorded on-chain.

Treasury records include:

* Recipient address
* Distribution amount
* Execution event data
* Transaction history

These records provide a publicly auditable history of treasury activity.

---

## Immutability

Treasury Module V1 is designed as transparent treasury infrastructure.

The contract executes predefined treasury distribution logic and does not possess discretionary authority over treasury funds.

Governance may authorize distributions according to protocol rules, but the Treasury Module itself cannot alter those rules after deployment.

---

## Role in LaborCoin

The Treasury Module serves as the financial execution layer of the LaborCoin ecosystem.

Its purpose is to:

* Hold treasury funds
* Execute approved treasury distributions
* Provide transparent on-chain accounting
* Enforce separation between governance decisions and treasury execution

By separating governance from treasury execution, LaborCoin maintains a clear and auditable distinction between decision-making and fund distribution.

All treasury activity remains publicly visible, verifiable, and subject to the rules established by the LaborCoin governance process.
