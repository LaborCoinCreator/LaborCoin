# LaborCoin Redpaper

## Introduction

If you have tried to answer the question, *“Why has there not been a general strike in the United States?”*, you eventually arrive at the same conclusion: economic retaliation prevents it.

Workers cannot afford to strike.

If you have looked at the crypto ecosystem and asked, *“What would a project look like if it were not extractive or fraudulent?”*, LaborCoin is an answer to that question.

If you have asked whether blockchain technology can be used to materially support the working class, LaborCoin is an attempt to do exactly that.

LaborCoin is a tool for coordination, solidarity, and collective action.

---

## The Problem

Economic exploitation persists because workers lack the ability to act collectively without immediate financial consequences.

* Workers depend on wages for survival
* Striking results in loss of income
* Benefits and support systems are often unavailable to striking workers
* Most individuals cannot withstand even short-term financial disruption

This creates a system where:

* Labor generates value
* Capital captures that value
* Workers are prevented from resisting

At the same time:

* Political systems are heavily influenced by concentrated wealth
* Labor organizing has weakened over time
* Institutional support for workers has declined

The result is a structural imbalance of power.

Workers cannot act, even when collective action would improve their conditions.

---

## The Opportunity

The only remaining leverage available to the working class is collective action.

The challenge is not willingness. It is coordination and survival.

LaborCoin is designed to address both.

It enables:

* Collective pooling of value
* Democratic allocation of resources
* Direct support for workers engaged in labor action

This creates a mechanism where workers can support each other without relying on centralized institutions.

---

## The System

LaborCoin consists of three primary components:

* **LABR** — a token used to build and represent collective value
* **LaborCoin DAO** — a governance system for allocating resources
* **LABRV** — a non-transferable voting token (one per participant)

Together, these components form a system for:

* Coordinating decisions
* Funding collective action
* Executing outcomes transparently

---

## How It Works

### 1. Building Collective Value

Participants can:

* Purchase and hold LABR
* Contribute directly to the DAO treasury

As participation grows, the collective pool of resources increases.

---

### 2. Supporting Workers

Workers preparing for collective action can:

* Acquire LABR
* Coordinate with others
* Prepare proposals for support

When workers take action, they can:

* Use their resources
* Receive support from the DAO treasury

---

### 3. Governance

Funding decisions are made through the DAO.

* Each participant receives **1 LABRV**
* LABRV is **non-transferable**
* Voting follows a **one person, one vote** model

Proposals must meet:

* **75% participation**
* **75% approval**

This ensures decisions reflect broad consensus.

---

### 4. Execution

When a proposal passes:

* The DAO executes the decision
* Funds are transferred through controlled contracts
* No centralized authority intervenes

Execution is:

* Transparent
* Automatic
* Enforced on-chain

---

## Strategy

The system enables a repeatable pattern:

1. Workers organize

5. Action is sustained

This model can be applied across:

* Individual workplaces

* Regions

Workers are not required to rely on formal unions or centralized organizations.

They can coordinate directly.
